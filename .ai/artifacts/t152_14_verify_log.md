## What shipped

Zooming into dense forest no longer loses the trees (operator **S7**). Two compounding defects fixed:

- **A2 — swap counted the wrong set.** The heatmap-vs-glyph swap fed `heatmap_trees()` with
  `exact_tree_count` (whole-chunk sums) — a 512 m chunk 1 % on-screen contributed 100 % of its
  trees, so a detail-zoom viewport over the Everon mega-forest tripped the 150 k budget and cleared
  every glyph. Now the swap counts **viewport-visible** trees via a new area-fraction
  `visible_tree_count`, with **hysteresis** `[0.85×budget, budget]` (enter > 150 000, stay until
  < 127 500) to kill pan flicker. `INSTANCE_BUDGET` / `TREE_GLYPH_MIN_ZOOM` / density RGBA unchanged.
- **A3 — forest-mass handoff hole.** Fill keyed off zoom alone (`class_visible("forestFill") = z<0`),
  so at z ≥ 0 the green mass was off even when glyphs were budget-cleared → blank band. New residency
  decision `forest_fill_effective()` keeps the mass alive at z ≥ 0 **while tree glyphs are
  heatmap-swapped or the lane packs empty**, and off once glyphs actually pack.
  `class_visible("forestFill")` stays the pure-zoom oracle (`lod_gates.rs` untouched).

## Property-gate ladder table

Captured from `property_never_blank_zoom_ladder` (synthetic dense-forest fixture, 9 draw chunks,
4 fully covered → `visible = 4 × per_chunk`). State is **constant across z ∈ {0, 0.5, …, 6}** because
the swap is count-driven and `forestFill` is pure-zoom-off for all z ≥ 0. Never-blank holds on every
row (glyphs ∨ fill ∨ heatmap+grid).

| Scenario | visible | z ∈ {0 … 6} | tree glyphs | forest mass (`forest_fill_effective`) | heatmap | density grid | blank? |
|----------|---------|-------------|-------------|----------------------------------------|---------|--------------|--------|
| Under budget (50/chunk) | 200 | all 13 steps | **✓ 450** | off | off | nonzero | **no** (glyphs) |
| Over budget (40 000/chunk) | 160 000 | all 13 steps | 0 (cleared) | **✓ persists** | **✓ on** | nonzero | **no** (mass + heatmap) |

Under budget → glyphs carry the band, mass off (correct T-151.5.1 handoff). Over budget → the
heatmap rung owns trees and the mass persists as the green fallback. No `(no glyphs ∧ no fill ∧ no
heatmap)` row exists ⇒ **no blank band at any detail zoom**.

## Scoped decision (LANGUAGE GATE — one thin call)

`forest_fill_effective` is wired to the **residency-owned land-cover mega-hull** (`pushLandcover`,
the single additive TS call). The **TBDD forest-mass controller** (`useWgpuForestMass.ts:204`) keeps
its existing `class_visible('forestFill', zoom)` gate — it holds no `WorldResidency` handle, so
consuming the getter there would need new plumbing = a second call, violating the gate. The coarse
land-cover hull covers the same forest regions and is the green layer that satisfies never-blank; and
with the area-fraction + hysteresis swap, glyphs stay on at detail zoom in the common case, so the
handoff is the mega-viewport safety net. Follow-up only if operator M1 finds the coarse hull
insufficient vs. the finer TBDD mass.
